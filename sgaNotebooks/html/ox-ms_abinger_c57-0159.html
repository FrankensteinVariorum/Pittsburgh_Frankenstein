I persued him;– & for many months this

pursuit has been my task. Guided by a slight clue
I followed the windings of the Rhone
but vainly – The mediterranean appeared
and by a strange chance I saw the fiend
enter by night & hide himself in a vessel
bound for the black sea – I followed him
I knew the
vessel in which
he was concealed
& he escaped me
I know not
how.
Amidst the wilds of Tartary &
Russia although he still evaded me
I have ever followed in his track – Some
times the peasants scared by his horrid
Apparition informed me of his path
Sometimes he himself who feared that
if I lost all trace I should despair & die
often left some mark to guide me –The
snows descended on my head & I saw the print
of his huge step on the white plain –To
