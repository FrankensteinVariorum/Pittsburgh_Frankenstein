In this retreat I devoted the mor
ning to labour, but in the evening,
when the weather permitted I walked
on the stony beach of the sea to listen
to the waves as they roared & dashed at
my feet; It was a monotonous yet
ever changing scene. I thought of Swit
zerland; It was far different from
this desolate & appalling landscape –Its
hills are covered with vines & its
cottages are scattered thickly in the
plains— Its fair lakes reflect a
blue & gentle sky & when troubled
by the winds it is but the play of
a lively infant when compared
to the roarings of the giant ocean.


Inthis manner I
distributed my occupations when I first
arrived; but as I proceeded in my work it
became every day more horrible & irksome
to me. Sometimes I could not prevail
on myself to enter my laboratory for several
days and at other times I toiled day &
night in order to complete it. It was indeed
a filthy work in which I was en-
gaged. During my first experiment a kind of
enthusiastic frenzy had blinded me to the
