I thank you, replied I, listen therefore to the
deposition that I have to make. It is indeed a tale
so strange that I should fear you would not
credit it were there not something in truth which how
ever wonderful forces conviction. The story is too con
nected to be mistaken for a dream and I have no
motive for falsehood. My manner as I thus addressed
him was impressive but calm; I had formed in my
own heart a resolution to pursue my destroyer to death
and this purpose quieted my agony and provisonably
