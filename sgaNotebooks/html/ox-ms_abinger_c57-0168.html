Walton in continuation.


August 13th
You have read this strange & terrific story,
Margaret and do not you feel your blood con
gealed with horror

like that
which even
now curdles
mine – Sometimes seized with sudden agony he
could not continue his tale, at others his voice broken
yet piercing uttered the words of which it is
composed. – His fine & lovely
eyes were now lighted up with indignation now subdued
to downcast sorrow & infinite wretchedness. Sometimes
he commanded his countenance & tones
and related the most horrible incidents
with a tranquil voice suppressing every
mark of agitation – then like a vol
cano bursting forth his face would sud
denly change to an expression of the
wildest rage as he shrieked

out his imprecations ^on his persecutor.


His tale is connected & told with
an appearance of the simplest truth
yet I own to you my sister that the
letters of Felix & Safie which he
shewed me & the apparition of the
monster seen from our ship brought
to me a greater conviction of the truth
of his narrative
than his asserverations—however earnest & connected. Such a monster
