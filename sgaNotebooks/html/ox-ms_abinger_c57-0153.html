What then became of me? I know
not—I lost sensation & chains & darkness were

the only objects that pressed upon me – some
times indeed I dreamed that I wandered
in flowery meadows & pleasant vales with
the friends of my youth – But I awoke &
found myself in a dungeon. Melancholy
followed but by degrees I regained a clear
conception of my miseries & situation &
was then released from my prison. For
they had called me mad & During many
months as I understood a solitary
cell had been my habitation. –
But liberty
