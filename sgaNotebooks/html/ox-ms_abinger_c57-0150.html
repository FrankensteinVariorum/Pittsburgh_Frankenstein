I pointed to where he had disappeared &

but in vain and passing several hours

I was exhausted; a film covered my
eyes and my skin was parched with the heat
of fever. In this state I lay on a bed hardly
conscious of what had happened & my eyes
wandered round the room as if to seek
somthing that I had lost. At length I remem
bered that my father would anxiously
expect the return of Elizabeth & myself
and that I must return alone. This
reflection brought tears into my eyes & I
wept for a long time. I reflected on my
misfortunes & their cause and was bewildered
