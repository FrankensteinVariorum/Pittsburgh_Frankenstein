From Rotterdam we went
by sea to England. It was on a clear
morning in the latter days of September
that I first saw the white cliffs of
Britain. The banks of the Thames presented
a new scene; they were flat but fertile
& almost every town was marked by
some story. We saw Tilbury Fort &
remembered the Spanish Armada;
Gravesend, Woolwich, Greenwich places
which I had heard of even in my
country. At length we saw the nume
rous steeples of London; St Pauls
towerering above all & the Tower famed
in English History.
