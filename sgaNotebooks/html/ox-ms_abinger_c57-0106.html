Begone, I replied; I do break my
promise; never will create another like
yourself, equal in deformity & wickedness.


Slave, said the wretch, I before
reasoned with you – but you have proved
yourself unworthy of my condescension. Remem
ber that I have power; you believe your
self miserable but I can make you so
wretched that the light of day will
be hateful to you. You are my creator
but I am your master; obey!


Wretch, said I, the hour of my weak-
ness is past & the period of your power is
arrived. Your threats cannot move me
to do an act of wickedness but they confirm
me in a resolution of not creating you
companion in vice. Shall I in cold blood
set loose upon the earth a dæmon
whose delight is in death & wretchedness.
Begone, I am firm and your words will
only exasperate my rage.


The Monster saw my determination
in my face and gnashed his teeth in the
impotence of anger. Shall each man, cried
he, find his equal, & each beast have
his mate and I be alone. I had feelings
of affection & they were returned by detes
tation. Man you may hate, but beware!
