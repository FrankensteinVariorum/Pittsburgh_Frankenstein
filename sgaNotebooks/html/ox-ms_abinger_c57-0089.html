most perpendicularly to the water
casting black & impenetrable shades
which would cause a gloomy & mournful
appearance were it not for the
most verdant islands that relieve
the eye by their gay appearance. I have
seen this lake agitated by a tempest
when the wind tore up whirlwinds
of water and gave you an idea of
what the waterspout must be on
the great ocean–and the waves dash
with fury on the base of the mountain
where the priest & his mistress
were overwhelmed by an avelanche,
& where their dying voices are still
said to be heard amid the pauses
of the night wind. I have seen the
mountains of la Valais & the
pays de Vaud; But this country, Victor,
pleases me more than all those wonders.
The Mountains of Switzerland are
more majestic & strange but there
is a charm in the banks of this divine
river that I never before saw equalled.
