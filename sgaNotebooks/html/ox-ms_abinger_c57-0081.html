I listened to my father in silence and remained
for sometime without offering any reply. I revolved
rapidly in my mind a multitude of thoughts and
endeavoured to come to some conclusion. Alas!
to me the idea of an immediate union with my
cousin was one of horror and dismay. I was bound by
a solemn promise which I had not yet fufilled and
dared not break; or if I did what manifold mise
ries might not impend over me and my devo
ted family! could I enter into a festival with
this deadly weight yet hanging round my neck and
bowing me to the ground.
