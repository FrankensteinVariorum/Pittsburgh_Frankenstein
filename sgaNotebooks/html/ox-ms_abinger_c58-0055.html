believe that I am he, whose thoughts were once
filled with sublime & transcendent visions of
the beauty & the majesty of goodness – But
it is even so – The fallen Angel becomes a
malignant devil – Yet even that enemy of
God & man had friends & associates in his
desolation. I am quite alone.


You, who call Frankenstein your friend, seem
to have a knowledge of my crimes & his
misfortunes. But in the detail which he
gave you of them he could not sum up the
hours & months of misery which I endured
wasting in impotent passions. For whilst I
destroyed his hopes, I did not satisfy my
own desires. They were forever ardent & craving.
