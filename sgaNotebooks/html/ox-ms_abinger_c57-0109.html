When it became noon & the sun rose
higher I lay down on the grass & was
overpowered by a deep sleep. I had been
awake the whole of the preceeding night,
my nerves were agitated & my eyes inflamed
with watching & misery. The sleep into which
I now sunk refreshed me & when I awoke
I again felt as if I belonged to a race of
human beings like myself & I began to
reflect upon what had passed with
greater composure —Yet still the words of
the fiend rung in my ears like a death
knell, they appeared like a dream, yet
distinct and oppressive as a reality.
