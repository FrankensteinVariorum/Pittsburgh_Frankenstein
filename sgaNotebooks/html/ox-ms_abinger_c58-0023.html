What then became of me? I know not; I
lost sensation and chains and darkness were the only
objects that pressed upon me. Sometimes indeed
I dreamt that I wandered in flowery meadows and
pleasant vales with the friends of my youth; but I
awoke and found myself in a dungeon. Melancholy
followed but by degrees. I gained a clear conception
of my miseries and situation and was then released
from my prison. For they had called me mad
and during many months, as I understood, a solita
