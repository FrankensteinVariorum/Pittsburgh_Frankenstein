Amidst the wilds of Tartary and Russia although
he still evaded me I have ever followed in his
tract. Sometimes the peasants scared by this horrid
apparition informed me of his path; sometimes
he himself, who feared that if I lost all trace I should
despair and die, often left some mark to guide
me. The snows descended on my head and I saw the print
of his huge step on the white plain. To you
first enterring on life to whom care is new
and agony unknown; how can you understand what
I have felt and still feel. Cold, want and fatigue were
the least pains which I was destined to endure; I was
