was still superior to thine: for the bitter sting
of remorse may not cease to Rankle in my wounds,
until death shall close them forever.


But soon, he cried with sad & solemn enthusi
asm, I shall die, & what I now feel be no
longer felt. . . Soon these burning miseries will
be extinct. I shall ascend my funeral pile triumphantly;
& exult in the agony of the torturing flames.
The light of that conflagration will fade away.
My ashes will be swept into the sea
by the winds. My spirit will sleep in peace;
or if it thinks will surely not think thus.
Adieu.


He sprung from the cabin-window, as he said this,
upon an ice raft which lay close to the vessel;
he was soon borne away by the waves, & lost
in the darkness of distance.
