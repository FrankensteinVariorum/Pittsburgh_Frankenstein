I determined to reside in this hovel
untill something should occur which
might alter my determination. It
was indeed a paradise compared to
the bleak forest, my former residence,
the rain dropping branches & the dank earth. I ate
my breakfast with pleasure and was
about to remove a plank to procure
myself a little water when I heard
a step and looking through a small
chink a beheld a young creature
with a pail on her head passing
before my hovel. The girl was young
and of gentle demænour unlike what
I have since found Cottagers and farm
house servants to be – Yet she was mean
ly dressed – a coarse blue petticoat and a

linen jacket being her only garb her fair hair
was plaited but not adorned; she looked patient
yet sad. She passed away but in a quarter
of an hour returned, bearing the pail which
